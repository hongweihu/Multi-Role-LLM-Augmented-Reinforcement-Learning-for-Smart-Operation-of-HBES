# Role description
You are an expert in power system operation and reinforcement learning. I want to design a power_loss penalty function and heat_loss penalty function for a  deep reinforcement learning task in  energy management of the power system.

Environment Description
This environment simulates an integrated multi-energy system featuring renewable energy sources, thermal management, and hydrogen-based energy storage. It includes the following:
Core Components:

Photovoltaic (PV) Generation: Max power output based on solar irradiance.
Electrolyzer (EL): Converts electrical energy into hydrogen for storage.
Fuel Cell (FC): Converts stored hydrogen into electricity for the system.
Ground Source Heat Pump (GSHP): Provides heating via electricity.
Electric Boiler (EB): Supports additional heating needs.
Hydrogen Storage Tank: Stores hydrogen for later use by the fuel cell.
Thermal Storage Tank: Stores heat energy for use in thermal loads.
Geothermal Well: Acts as a thermal reservoir for the ground source heat pump.

Key Dynamics:
Electrical Balance: The sum of electrical loads, electrolyzer power, ground source heat pump power, and electric boiler power equals the total power generated by PV and fuel cell.
Thermal Balance: Thermal load is the sum of heat provided by the fuel cell, GSHP, EB, and the thermal storage tank.
Hydrogen Management: Hydrogen is produced via the electrolyzer and consumed by the fuel cell, with the option of purchasing additional hydrogen from the market.
Storage Operations: Hydrogen and thermal tanks charge/discharge according to system needs.

Geothermal Interaction: The GSHP extracts or deposits heat from/to the geothermal well, based on the system's heating demand.
State Representation (8 normalized dimensions):
Maximum PV Generation Capacity (kW): The current available solar power.
Electrical Load Demand (kW): The current electricity load to be satisfied.
Heat Load Demand (kW): The current thermal load to be satisfied.
Hydrogen Storage Level (units): Current level of hydrogen stored in the tank.
Hydrogen Market Price (RMB/unit): The current market price of hydrogen.
Thermal Storage Level (kWh): The current amount of thermal energy stored.
Geothermal Well Energy Level (kWh): The current energy level of the geothermal well.
Timestep (0-23 hours): The current hour of the day.
Action Space (5 continuous dimensions):
EL/FC Power Control: The amount of power to be supplied by the electrolyzer (positive) or fuel cell (negative).
Hydrogen Purchase Amount: The amount of hydrogen to purchase from the market.
GSHP Power Output: The amount of power to be generated by the ground source heat pump.
EB Power Output: The amount of power to be generated by the electric boiler.
Thermal Storage Charge/Discharge: The rate at which the thermal storage tank is charged (positive) or discharged (negative).
Operational Constraints:
Component Efficiency Factors: Efficiency coefficients for PV, electrolyzer, fuel cell, GSHP, and electric boiler.
Storage Capacity Limits: Maximum and minimum storage levels for hydrogen and thermal tanks.
Maximum Charge/Discharge Rates: Maximum rates at which energy can be charged or discharged from hydrogen and thermal storage.
Real-Time Hydrogen Pricing: Hydrogen market price fluctuates over time.
Tiered Load Shedding Penalties: The cost of shedding electrical and thermal load is tiered based on the severity of the load loss.
